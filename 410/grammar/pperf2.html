Present Perfect 2: Irregular Past Participles

To make the present perfect, you need to use HAVE + PAST PARTICIPLE. The past participles of most verbs are formed using -ED, but some common verbs have unusual past participles. In this exercise, you can test your knowledge of irregular past participles.

Click on the button beside the correct answer.

 
  1. Begin: We're late -- the game has already ______.
    1.   begin
    2.   begun
    3.   began
  2. Break: Shelley can't go skiing any more. She has ______ her leg.
    1.   broken
    2.   breaken
    3.   broke
  3. Become: In the last few years, it has ______ more and more difficult to get into college.
    1.   become
    2.   became
    3.   becume
  4. Bring: Duncan's here, and he's ______ some sandwiches!
    1.   braught
    2.   brung
    3.   brought
  5. Choose: She's getting married in a week, and she hasn't ______ her dress yet.
    1.   choosen
    2.   chose
    3.   chosen
  6. Drink: It's so hot that I've ______ five cans of Coke already this afternoon.
    1.   drank
    2.   drunk
    3.   drunken
  7. Eat: You've ______ my lunch! How dare you!
    1.   eaten
    2.   ate
    3.   aten
  8. Fall: The price of oil has ______ rapidly since January.
    1.   fell
    2.   felt
    3.   fallen
  9. Feel: I've never ______ so good!
    1.   felt
    2.   feelen
    3.   fold
  10. Hear: Have you ______ about the accident?
    1.   heared
    2.   hearn
    3.   heard
  11. Lose: I've ______ my grammar book. Have you seen it?
    1.   lost
    2.   loose
    3.   losen
  12. Put: I found your grammar book under the table. I've ______ it away on the bookshelf.
    1.   put
    2.   putten
    3.   pought
  13. Run: Jan's out of breath because she's ______ all the way from the bus terminal.
    1.   ran
    2.   run
    3.   runnen
  14. Sell: Have you ______ your car yet?
    1.   sellen
    2.   sole
    3.   sold
  15. Show: Stewart hasn't ______ up yet. He must have been delayed.
    1.   shown
    2.   shew
    3.   showed
  16. Sleep: I was so exhausted after the exam that I ______ for twelve hours.
    1.   sleep
    2.   sleeped
    3.   slept
  17. Steal: Someone has ______ my bicycle!
    1.   stolen
    2.   stole
    3.   stelt
  18. Swim: She's never ______ in the sea before.
    1.   swim
    2.   swum
    3.   swam
  19. Teach: Gerry has ______ English at UVic for many years.
    1.   tought
    2.   taught
    3.   teach
  20. Write: How many pages have you ______ for your homework?
    1.   writen
    2.   wrote
    3.   written
