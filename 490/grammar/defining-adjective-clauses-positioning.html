Defining Adjective Clauses — Positioning

Where do you put adjective clauses?

You can use an adjective clause after any noun in the sentence.

  • It can be part of the subject.

    People who have guns scare me.

  • It can be part of the object.

    I don't like people who have guns.

  • It can be part of a phrase.

    There's a guy in the class that I took last year who knows you.
